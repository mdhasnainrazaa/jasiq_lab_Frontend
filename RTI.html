RTI Overview

What is Right to Information?

The Right to Information Act, 2005 empowers every citizen of India to seek information from Public Authorities. It aims to promote transparency and accountability in the working of every public authority.

Key Features:

  • Every citizen has the right to seek information from public authorities
  • Information must be provided within 30 days (48 hours for life and liberty matters)
  • Nominal fee structure for obtaining information
  • Appeal mechanism available for unsatisfactory responses
  • Penalties for non-compliance by public officials

How to Apply for RTI

Online Application

Steps to Apply Online:

  • Visit the official RTI portal
  • Register with your basic details
  • Fill the RTI application form
  • Pay the prescribed fee online
  • Submit the application
  • Note down the application number for tracking

Offline Application

Steps to Apply Offline:

  • Download RTI application form
  • Fill the form with required details
  • Attach demand draft/postal order for fee
  • Submit at the concerned office
  • Obtain acknowledgment receipt
  • Track your application status

Fee Structure

RTI Fee Details

Application Fee:

  • RTI Application: ₹10/-
  • First Appeal: ₹10/-
  • Second Appeal: ₹10/-
  • BPL Citizens: No fee

Additional Charges:

  • Photocopying: ₹2 per page
  • Certified Copy: ₹5 per page
  • CD/DVD: ₹50 per disc
  • Inspection: First hour free, ₹5 for each additional hour
